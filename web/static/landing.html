How CipherVault Works

Security through transparency. Here's exactly what happens when you access your vault — no black boxes.

You enter your master password

Your master password is processed locally. It never leaves your browser, never hits a network request, never gets logged anywhere.

Argon2id derives your key

The password is transformed into a 256-bit cryptographic key using Argon2id — intentionally slow and memory-intensive to defeat brute force attacks.

AES-256-GCM decrypts the vault

Your encrypted vault blob is decrypted in-browser using the derived key. The authenticated encryption scheme verifies no tampering occurred.

Vault is live — data stays local

Your decrypted entries exist only in browser memory. Every save re-encrypts immediately. Lock the vault and everything is wiped from memory instantly.

Trust Model

Designed to Distrust Us

CipherVault's security model assumes the worst: that servers get breached, admins go rogue, and networks are compromised. You're still safe.

  • 🛡️
    Server-Side Blindness

    Our servers only store encrypted ciphertext. Without your master password — which we never receive — it is indistinguishable from random noise.

  • ⚡
    Authenticated Encryption

    GCM mode provides both confidentiality and integrity. Any tampering with the encrypted payload is detected and rejected before decryption.

  • 🌊
    Quantum Resistance

    Argon2id's memory-hardness provides meaningful resistance against future quantum computing attacks on the key derivation layer.

  • 👁️
    No Analytics, No Tracking

    We don't track what passwords you store, how many entries you have, or which sites you use. Your usage patterns are as private as your passwords.

Frequently Asked Questions

Everything you need to know about our zero-knowledge architecture, security guarantees, and recovery mechanisms.

What is "Zero-Knowledge" encryption?

Zero-Knowledge means that the server (and the developers) have absolutely zero knowledge of what your passwords are. Your Master Password is never sent to our servers; it only exists temporarily on your own device to lock and unlock your data. If our database is hacked, the hackers only get mathematically unbreakable gibberish.

What happens if I forget my Master Password?

You lose your data. Because we practice strict Zero-Knowledge security, we cannot reset your password or recover your vault. Your Master Password is the only mathematical key that can unlock your data.

Is my data safe if the server is hacked?

Yes. Your vault is encrypted using AES-256-GCM, the same encryption standard used by governments and militaries worldwide. Without your specific Master Password, the world's most powerful supercomputers would take millions of years to crack it.

Why do I need to log in with Google and a Master Password?

Logging in with Google proves your identity to our servers and helps us fetch the correct encrypted file. The Master Password proves your authorization to unlock the file. Splitting these two steps prevents bots from spamming our servers with fake passwords.
